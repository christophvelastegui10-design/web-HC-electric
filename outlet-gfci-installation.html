Service Specific FAQ

A GFCI (Ground Fault Circuit Interrupter) outlet detects tiny imbalances in current flow — like current leaking through a person — and cuts power in milliseconds to prevent electrocution. They are required by NEC code near any water source: kitchens, bathrooms, garages, outdoors, and basements. Many older homes are missing GFCI protection in several of these areas.
